My Life Story

Writing Level A2 25 min

Think about someone in your family: a parent, a grandparent, an aunt or uncle. They have a story. They were born somewhere, went to school, got a job, maybe moved to another city. In English, we call these life events, and writing about them in a clear, connected way is an important skill.

In this activity, you will learn to use three key connectors: in, when, and later to link life events together and create a story that flows naturally from one moment to the next.

You will practise with a model story, do controlled exercises, and then write your own life story about a family member, using he or she, without naming the person. This is a skill you can use immediately in real life: talking about your family, writing a biography, or even introducing someone in English.

Learning Objective

Write a short life story using in, when, and later to link events chronologically.

Three Ways to Link Events

In English, we use three key words to connect life events in a story. Study the table, then look at how they appear in the model story below.

Word Use it with... Examples
in Years and months
→ a specific point in time		 In 1991, he was born in Ankara.
In September, she started her new job.
when Ages or events
→ two things happening together		 When he was 11, he moved to Istanbul.
When she left school, she found a job.
later After a time expression
→ connects two separate sentences		 She got a job. A year later, she got married.
He left school. Two years later, he went to university.
Model Story — Okan

In 1991, Okan was born in Ankara, Turkey. When he was 11, his family moved to Istanbul and he went to school there.

He left school in 2009 and got a job in a bank. A year later, he went to university in Istanbul and studied engineering.

In 2013, he met Carolina. Two years later, they got married. In 2016, Okan got a job as an engineer in the UK and they moved there together.

in + year / month
when + age / event
time expression + later
Tip
  • Use "in" before a year: "In 2005". Use "when" before an age or an event: "When he was 18" or "When she left school".
  • "Later" always comes after a time word: "A month later", "Three years later" — never at the start on its own.

Sample Story

In 1955, he was born in a small village in Italy. When he was 18, he moved to Rome to study architecture. He loved living in the big city.

In 1980, he finished university and started his first job. Three years later, he met his wife at a party. They got married in 1985.

A few years later, they moved to the countryside and had two children. Now, he is retired and enjoys gardening.

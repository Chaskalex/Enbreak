Drafting an Essay Introduction

Writing Level A2+ 25 min

First impressions count. In academic writing, your introduction is the first part your reader encounters. It serves as a map that guides the reader from a general topic toward the specific goal of your work.

In this activity, you will explore the typical inverted triangle model of an academic introduction, moving from general context and common knowledge to a specific thesis statement. You will learn how to use connectors of sequence and formal vocabulary to present your ideas logically.

Through analysis and practice, you will build the skills to draft introductory paragraphs that are clear, professional, and effectively communicate the purpose of your academic projects or essays.

Learning Objective

Structure an academic introduction following a general-to-specific pattern using connectors of sequence.

1

The Inverted Triangle

Also known as the funnel model, this structure guides the reader from a broad topic to a specific argument through three key stages.

Step 1
Open with a Hook
Introduce the topic. Engage the reader with a striking fact, a question, a concession, or a quotation.
Step 2
Build the Context
Provide background: accepted facts, current trends, or a brief review of the existing conversation on the topic.
Step 3
State Your Thesis
State the specific, arguable claim your essay will develop and defend.
Tip

Context and background together are typically longer than the thesis, but the thesis is the most important sentence of the paragraph.

2

Hook Strategies

The opening of your introduction must generate interest. There is no single correct approach, but certain strategies are particularly effective in academic writing.

Statistic
"Despite comprising less than 5% of the world's population, the United States accounts for nearly 25% of global carbon emissions."
Thought-Provoking Question
"What does it mean to be literate in an age where most information is consumed through images and short video clips?"
Relevant Quotation
"As George Orwell famously observed, 'Political language is designed to make lies sound truthful', a warning that remains strikingly relevant today."
Concession
"Many people think that working from home makes employees lazy. However, recent data shows that remote workers are actually more productive."
Contextual Definition
"The term 'digital divide' refers not merely to access to technology, but to the unequal ability to use it in ways that generate social and economic capital."
Anecdote or Scenario
"During my first year of university, I noticed that most students were sleeping less than five hours a night. This personal observation made me question the impact of academic stress on health."
Tip

Avoid overused openers: Dictionary definitions ("According to the Oxford Dictionary...") are a common but weak strategy. They are acceptable only when you reinterpret the definition or challenge it in some way.

3

Language for Background Information

Use these formal structures to introduce context, trends, and accepted knowledge before you state your thesis.

Function Formal Phrase Example in Context
Accepted knowledge It is well known that …climate change is a serious danger to coastal areas.
Growing trend In recent years, there has been a growing interest in …how informal learning helps professionals.
Identifying a gap However, not much attention has been given to …how these rules affect rural communities.
Scholarly consensus Studies consistently show that …early childhood nutrition affects brain development.
Ongoing debate It is still debated whether …limiting screen time is truly effective.
Contextual shift In this context, …remote work has become a permanent feature of modern cities.
4

The Thesis Statement

The thesis statement is the most important sentence in your essay. It must make a specific, arguable claim, not just state an obvious fact.

Weak Thesis

"Social media has changed the way we communicate."

This is an obvious fact. No one can reasonably disagree with it, so it cannot drive an argument.

Strong Thesis

"Social media makes society more divided because algorithms only show users opinions they already agree with."

Specific, arguable, and implies a line of evidence the essay will develop.

Use these verbs and structures to introduce your thesis formally:

Arguing
"This paper argues that…"
Examining
"This essay examines how…"
Challenging
"This study challenges the assumption that…"
Proposing
"This paper proposes that…"

Option A:  Social Media & Mental Health
A2+ Sample

Despite the fact that young people today are more connected than any previous generation, rates of loneliness and anxiety among teenagers have risen sharply over the past two decades. According to recent data, one in seven young people globally experiences a mental health disorder, with daily digital media consumption identified as a significant factor. In recent years, researchers have tried to understand exactly how social media platforms influence psychological wellbeing. This paper argues that compulsive social media use, specifically through comparing oneself to others online, is a major risk factor for depressive symptoms that schools and parents must urgently address.

Concession hook: Acknowledges an opposing idea before narrowing
Background: Accepted data and current research trends
Thesis: Specific, arguable claim that implies evidence
Option B:  Artificial Intelligence & Professional Learning
A2+ Sample

When a junior doctor checks a patient's symptoms using an AI tool instead of asking a senior colleague, the boundary between human expertise and machine assistance becomes very unclear. The rapid entry of artificial intelligence into the workplace has generated considerable debate about the future of jobs and how workers will learn new skills. Research consistently demonstrates that companies using AI-assisted training programs achieve much better results than those relying on traditional methods. In this context, this paper proposes that the most effective way to train professionals today is to treat human-machine collaboration as a learning opportunity rather than a threat to job security.

Scenario hook: Places the reader in a specific, vivid situation
Background: Contextual debate and empirical evidence
Thesis: Reframes the debate in a clear, arguable way
Option C:  The Four-Day Work Week
A2+ Sample

What would happen if companies paid employees their full salary to work only four days a week instead of five? This question has gained serious attention recently, as workers increasingly report feeling burned out and overworked. It is well known in human resources research that employee wellbeing directly affects company performance; yet, many businesses still stick to the traditional five-day schedule. This essay contends that shifting to a four-day work week not only drastically improves employee mental health but also leads to higher overall productivity for modern companies.

Question hook: Engages the reader by posing a real dilemma
Background: Recent trends and established research
Thesis: Makes a strong, arguable claim about business practices

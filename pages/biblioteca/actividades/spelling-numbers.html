Number Dictation

Vocabulary Level A1 20 min

Numbers are everywhere in our daily lives. From phone numbers and prices to dates and addresses, being able to understand and say numbers correctly is essential for effective communication.

In English, there are specific rules for pronouncing large numbers, money, and decimals. Mastering these will help you sound more natural and avoid misunderstandings.

In this activity, we will review numbers from zero to millions, learn about money symbols, and practice with real-world examples.

Learning Objective

You will be able to recognize, pronounce, and write numbers, prices, and amounts correctly.

Skill Focus

Vocabulary & Listening (A1 Level)

1

Numbers 0 - 10

Click on each number to hear its pronunciation.

The number 0 is usually pronounced as "zero". However, when saying phone numbers, room numbers, or years, it is often pronounced like the letter "oh".

2

Numbers 11 - 19

Pay attention to the "-teen" ending.

Tip
s="tip-content">

Numbers like 13, 14, 15 end in -teen (stressed). Numbers like 30, 40, 50 end in -ty (unstressed). Be careful not to confuse 15 (fifteen) with 50 (fifty)!

3

Numbers 20 - 90

Counting by tens.

4

Large Numbers

Hundreds, thousands, and millions.

5

Money & Symbols

Common currency symbols and how to read prices.

Tip

For prices with decimals, we usually say the currency first, then the main number, and then the cents.
Example: $10.50 is read as "ten dollars fifty".
